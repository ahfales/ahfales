about me

Hello, my name is Adam Fales, and I am a PhD student at UChicago, where I study nineteenth-century US literature, with a focus on the histories of race, gender, and sexuality. I am also an associate editor at Chicago Review. I also write for both academic and public-facing publications.

To get in touch, email me or follow me on Twitter.


I am currently in my second year in the PhD in English language and literature. I also participate in the eighteenth and nineteenth century Atlantic cultures workshop, the book history reading group, and am one of the social organizers for the English department. You can find me on the department's website here.

My research methodology spans the fields of literary theory, literary history, and book history and material textual studies. I aim to develop a historicist approach that is at once attuned to the broad changes in nineteenth-century American culture and society as well as the way those changes were experienced on an individual level. I am currently pursuing projects on serial fiction by E.D.E.N. Southworth as it relates to the American family and reading public, another project on narratives about male sex workers from the nineteenth to the twenty-first centuries to understand the intimacies present in labor, and a study of William Wells Brown's novel Clotel as it relates to changes in US citizenship in the last half of the nineteenth century. With Jordan Alexander Stein, I have also published an article (Leviathan, 2019) on Elizabeth Shaw Melville and the work that she did to aid the career of her husband Herman. I have presented my work at The Melville Society Conference, University of Michigan, Northwestern University, The McNeil Center at Penn, and UChicago's Eighteenth- and Nineteenth-century Atlantic Cultures Workshop.
Chicago Review publishes critical and creative work related to contemporary avant-garde literature. You can find more information about CR here.
